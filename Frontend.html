1. what is stock market?

The stock market is where companies sell shares of stock to investors to raise capital. These shares give shareholders voting rights and a claim on earnings through capital gains and dividends. Individual and institutional investors buy and sell shares on stock exchanges, creating a public market. When purchasing a share, investors buy it from an existing shareholder, not the company. Selling a share also involves finding another investor to buy it on the exchange, as the company doesn't buy back shares. The stock market facilitates the flow of capital between investors and companies, providing a crucial source of funding for businesses.

2. what are high and low prices?

High and low prices in the stock market refer to the highest and lowest prices at which a particular stock has traded over a certain period. These prices are influenced by a variety of factors, including company performance, economic indicators, investor sentiment, and global events. High prices reflect positive investor sentiment and strong company performance, while low prices indicate negative sentiment or poor company performance. Tracking a stock's high and low prices over time can provide insight into its volatility, potential for growth, and overall market trends. Investors use this information to make informed decisions about buying, selling, or holding stocks in their portfolios.

3. what are opening and closing prices in stock market?

Opening and closing prices in the stock market refer to the prices at which a particular stock begins and ends trading on a given day. The opening price is determined by the first trade of the day, while the closing price is determined by the last trade of the day. These prices can be influenced by a variety of factors, including company performance, economic indicators, investor sentiment, and global events. The difference between the opening and closing prices is known as the daily price range, and investors track this information to identify market trends and potential opportunities for buying or selling stocks. The opening and closing prices are key data points used by traders and investors to make informed decisions in the stock market.
